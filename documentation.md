1. Introduction to Medisynex Billing Software
Medisynex is a comprehensive, cloud-enabled billing and business management software designed specifically for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), retail shops, wholesalers, distributors, and service-based businesses across India and South Asia. Medisynex is purpose-built for the healthcare and medical supply industry, combining pharmacy billing, medical inventory, and GST compliance in one powerful platform.

Medisynex streamlines every aspect of your business operations — from creating professional GST-compliant invoices and managing inventory, to tracking payments, filing tax returns, and analysing business performance — all from a single unified platform.

1.1 Key Highlights
GST-compliant invoicing with auto tax calculation
Real-time inventory and stock management
Complete accounts receivable and payable tracking
GSTR-1, GSTR-2, and GSTR-3B report generation
Multi-user access with role-based permissions
Works on Windows, Mac, Android, and iOS
Cloud sync and offline mode support
Bank reconciliation and payment gateway integration

Who Is Medisynex For?
Medisynex is ideal for retailers, wholesalers, traders, manufacturers, service providers, pharmacies, restaurants, kirana stores, and any GST-registered business that needs to manage sales, purchases, inventory, and accounting in one place.

3. Account Creation & Registration
Before using Medisynex, every user must create a business account. The registration process captures essential business details required for GST compliance and invoice generation.

3.1 Step-by-Step Registration Process
Step 1 — Launch the Application
Open Medisynex on your device. You will be greeted by the Welcome Screen showing two options: Create New Account and Login to Existing Account.

Step 2 — Click 'Create New Account'
Tap or click the Create New Account button to begin the registration wizard.

Step 3 — Enter Business Information
Business Name (e.g., Sharma Traders Pvt. Ltd.)
Business Type: Retailer / Wholesaler / Manufacturer / Service Provider / Other
Business Category (e.g., Grocery, Electronics, Pharmacy, Clothing)
Mobile Number — used for OTP verification and login
Email Address — for account recovery and reports
Password — minimum 8 characters with at least one number

Step 4 — GST & Tax Information
GSTIN (GST Identification Number) — 15-digit number, if registered
PAN Number — optional but recommended
Composition Scheme: Yes / No
State and Pin Code of the registered business address

Step 5 — OTP Verification
An OTP (One-Time Password) is sent to your registered mobile number. Enter the OTP within 5 minutes to verify your phone number. On successful verification, your account is created.

Step 6 — Complete Business Profile
After verification, you are redirected to complete your business profile. This includes uploading your business logo, entering your full business address, bank account details, and configuring the invoice template.

Important
Your GSTIN is used to auto-populate HSN/SAC codes and calculate GST at the correct rate (CGST + SGST for intra-state or IGST for inter-state). Make sure it is entered correctly.

6. Sales & Invoicing
The Sales module is the most frequently used feature of Medisynex. It handles the complete lifecycle of a sale — from generating an invoice to tracking payment receipt.

6.1 Creating a New Sales Invoice
Step 1 — Navigate to Sales
From the Dashboard, click the Sales menu in the left sidebar or tap the New Sale quick button. This opens the New Invoice screen.

Step 2 — Select or Add a Customer
Type the customer's name, mobile number, or GSTIN in the Customer search box.
If the customer exists, their details auto-fill.
If the customer is new, click Add New Customer and fill in their Name, Mobile, GST Number, Address, and State.
For walk-in customers with no account, select the default Cash / Walk-In Customer option.

Step 3 — Set Invoice Details
Invoice Number — auto-generated sequentially (e.g., INV-2025-0001). Can be manually overridden.
Invoice Date — defaults to today; can be changed for backdated entries.
Due Date — for credit invoices, set the payment due date.
Place of Supply — auto-set based on customer's state; determines IGST vs CGST+SGST.

Step 4 — Add Items to the Invoice
Click Add Item or scan the product barcode.
Search for an item by name, SKU, or barcode.
The item's rate, unit, GST rate, and HSN code auto-populate from the product master.
Enter the Quantity. The line total is calculated automatically.
Apply item-level discount if applicable (flat amount or percentage).
Add multiple items by repeating the process. Reorder items by drag-and-drop.

Step 5 — Apply Discounts & Charges
Invoice-level discount (percentage or flat amount applied on the subtotal).
Additional charges: Shipping, Packing, Handling (each can be GST-applicable or not).
Round-off: Medisynex auto-rounds the final amount to the nearest rupee.

Step 6 — Review Tax Summary
A tax summary table is displayed before saving, showing: Taxable Amount, CGST, SGST, IGST, and Total Tax. This is auto-calculated based on each item's GST rate and the customer's state.

Step 7 — Payment Terms
Cash — full payment collected immediately.
Credit — customer pays later. Enter due date.
Partial — customer pays part now, rest later. Enter amount received.
Bank Transfer / UPI — record digital payment with reference number.

Step 8 — Save & Share
Click Save Invoice to record the sale.
Share instantly via WhatsApp, Email, or print a physical copy.
The invoice is saved in PDF format and stored in the cloud.

6.2 Invoice Formats Supported
Format	Description
Tax Invoice	Standard GST invoice for registered businesses. Mandatory for B2B sales above ₹200.
Bill of Supply	For GST-exempt goods or composition dealers (no GST charged).
Proforma Invoice	Preliminary invoice sent before actual sale. Not a legal document.
Delivery Challan	Goods dispatch note without a sale — for loans, transfers, or job work.
Credit Note	Issued when goods are returned or excess billed. Reduces the customer's balance.
Debit Note	Issued when additional amount is charged after the original invoice.
Quotation / Estimate	A price quote sent to customers. Can be converted to invoice in one click.

6.3 Sales Order Management
Before generating a final invoice, you can create a Sales Order (SO) to book a customer's order. Once the order is ready to dispatch, convert the SO to a Tax Invoice with a single click. SOs help track pending orders and manage production or procurement accordingly.

6.4 Recurring Invoices
For subscription-based or regular customers, set up Auto-Recurring Invoices. Configure the frequency (weekly, monthly, quarterly), start date, and end date. Medisynex will automatically generate and optionally email the invoice on the due date.

6.5 E-Invoice (IRN Generation)
For businesses with turnover above the e-invoicing threshold, Medisynex integrates with the government IRP (Invoice Registration Portal) to auto-generate the Invoice Reference Number (IRN) and QR code. The IRN is embedded in the PDF invoice automatically after government validation.

6.6 E-Way Bill
For goods transport above ₹50,000 in value, Medisynex generates an E-Way Bill by connecting to the government E-Way Bill portal. You can generate, update, cancel, and print the E-Way Bill directly from within the invoice screen without logging in to the government portal separately.

7. Purchase Management
The Purchase module helps you record all goods and services procured from suppliers, manage credit with vendors, and keep stock levels updated automatically.

7.1 Recording a Purchase Bill
Go to Purchases > New Purchase from the menu.
Select or add the Supplier (Vendor) with their GST number and address.
Enter the Supplier's Invoice Number and Invoice Date (mandatory for ITC claim).
Add purchased items, quantities, rates, and GST rates.
Set payment terms: Cash paid, Credit, or Partial payment.
Save the bill — stock is automatically incremented.

7.2 Purchase Order (PO)
Create Purchase Orders to send to suppliers before the goods arrive. Once the supplier delivers and you receive the bill, simply convert the PO to a Purchase Bill in one click. Track PO status: Pending, Partially Received, or Fully Received.

7.3 Purchase Returns
When goods are returned to a supplier (due to defects, wrong delivery, etc.), record a Purchase Return (Debit Note). This automatically reduces stock and creates a credit in the supplier's account, reducing the amount you owe them.

7.4 Expenses
Record non-purchase business expenses such as rent, electricity, salary, repairs, and other operational costs under the Expenses section. You can categorise each expense (e.g., Office, Transport, Marketing) and attach receipts or images as proof.

8. Inventory & Stock Management
Medisynex's inventory system gives you complete control over your products — their details, pricing, stock levels, and movements. Every sale and purchase automatically updates inventory in real time.

8.1 Adding Products/Items
Go to Inventory > Items > Add New Item. Fill in the following details:

Item Name	Full product name as it should appear on invoices.
Item Code / SKU	Your internal product code or barcode number.
Category	Product category (e.g., Electronics, FMCG, Clothing).
HSN / SAC Code	Mandatory for GST. Auto-suggested based on item name.

The Stock Summary screen shows all items with their current quantity, value (at purchase price), and value (at selling price). You can filter by category, apply search, and export to Excel.

8.3 Stock Adjustment
For physical stock takes or damage write-offs, use Stock Adjustment. Select the item, enter the actual physical count, choose the reason (Damaged, Lost, Theft, Audit Adjustment), and save. The system records the difference and updates stock accordingly.

8.4 Batch & Expiry Tracking
For businesses dealing with perishables, pharmaceuticals, or FMCG — Medisynex supports Batch Number, Manufacturing Date, and Expiry Date tracking. The system warns you before an expiry date approaches and ensures FIFO (First In First Out) stock disposal.

8.5 Multi-Warehouse / Multi-Location
If your business operates from multiple locations (main shop, godown, branch), you can create separate warehouses in Medisynex and track stock at each location independently. Transfer stock between locations using the Stock Transfer feature, which records the movement and updates both location balances.

8.6 Barcode & QR Code
Print barcodes and QR code labels for any item directly from Medisynex. Connect a barcode scanner for lightning-fast item entry during billing — no need to type product names. Supports standard barcode formats including Code 128, EAN-13, and QR codes.

10.3 UPI & Payment Gateway Integration
Medisynex integrates with popular UPI apps and payment gateways. Generate a UPI payment link or QR code from any invoice and share it with the customer. When the customer pays, the payment is automatically reconciled against the invoice (with some gateway integrations).

10.4 Cheque Management

11. Accounting & Ledger
Medisynex includes a complete double-entry accounting system. Even if you are not an accountant, the software manages all accounting entries automatically in the background for every transaction.

11.1 Chart of Accounts
The Chart of Accounts is the master list of all financial accounts in your business, organised into five main groups:
Assets — Cash, Bank, Stock, Debtors, Fixed Assets
Liabilities — Creditors, Loans, GST Payable
Income — Sales, Other Income
Expenses — Purchases, Wages, Rent, Utilities, etc.
Capital — Owner's Capital and Drawings
You can add custom ledger accounts under any group for specific tracking needs.

11.2 Journal Entries
For adjustments, opening entries, depreciation, or any entry that does not fit a standard transaction type, use Journal Vouchers. Enter debit and credit accounts with amounts and narration. The system enforces balanced double-entry (debit = credit).

11.3 Trial Balance
View the Trial Balance at any point to see the net balance of every account in the system. The Trial Balance confirms that your books are arithmetically correct — total debits equal total credits.

11.4 Profit & Loss Statement (P&L)
The Profit & Loss report shows your business's financial performance over a period. It includes Gross Profit (Sales minus Cost of Goods Sold) and Net Profit (Gross Profit minus all Operating Expenses). Filter by date range and compare two periods side by side.

11.5 Balance Sheet
The Balance Sheet is a snapshot of your business's financial position as of a specific date. It shows all Assets on one side and Liabilities + Capital on the other — they must always be equal. Medisynex generates the Balance Sheet automatically from all recorded transactions.

11.6 Cash Flow Statement
Track how money flows in and out of your business across three activities: Operating (day-to-day business), Investing (buying/selling assets), and Financing (loans, capital). A positive cash flow means your business generates more cash than it spends.

11.7 Bank Reconciliation
Bank Reconciliation matches your Medisynex bank ledger with your actual bank statement. Import your bank statement (CSV/Excel) or manually enter transactions. Medisynex highlights matching and unmatched entries, helping you identify errors, missing entries, or bank charges. Reconcile month-by-month to maintain accurate books.

12. GST Compliance & Tax Filings
GST compliance is one of Medisynex's strongest features. The software is built from the ground up to handle India's GST framework — from automatic tax calculation on every invoice to generating ready-to-file GST returns.

12.1 Automatic GST Calculation
When you create an invoice or purchase bill, Medisynex automatically calculates:
CGST + SGST for intra-state transactions (buyer and seller in the same state)
IGST for inter-state transactions (buyer and seller in different states)
No GST for exempt goods/services or composition scheme transactions
The correct tax rate is pulled from the item master (HSN/SAC code and rate). No manual calculation is required.

12.2 GSTR-1 Report
GSTR-1 is the monthly/quarterly return of outward supplies (your sales). Medisynex auto-populates GSTR-1 from all sales invoices recorded in the period. The report is organised into the correct sections: B2B (business to business), B2C (business to consumer), Credit Notes, and Amended invoices. Export as JSON and upload directly to the GST Portal, or use Medisynex's direct filing integration.

12.3 GSTR-2B Reconciliation
Download your GSTR-2B (auto-drafted ITC statement) from the GST Portal and import it into Medisynex. Medisynex will automatically match each entry with your recorded purchase bills, flag mismatches, and show unmatched invoices — helping you maximise your Input Tax Credit (ITC) claims and avoid excess claims.

12.4 GSTR-3B Summary
GSTR-3B is the monthly summary return where you declare total sales, total ITC, and pay the net GST liability. Medisynex generates the GSTR-3B auto-fill data based on your transactions, showing how much GST you owe after deducting eligible ITC. Review and file from Medisynex's GST Filing section.

12.5 Other GST Reports
GSTR-4 — for Composition Scheme dealers (quarterly).
GSTR-9 — Annual Return. Medisynex generates the annual compilation.
HSN Summary — break-up of sales by HSN code (mandatory in GSTR-1).
GST Payable vs. Paid Ledger — track your tax liability month by month.
Input Tax Credit (ITC) Register — track all ITC available and utilised.

18. Troubleshooting Common Issues
18.1 Cannot Login
Ensure your mobile number/email is correct.
Check internet connectivity (for cloud accounts).
Use Forgot Password to reset credentials.
If OTP is not received, check if your phone number has DND (Do Not Disturb) restrictions on promotional SMS. Try email login instead.

18.2 Invoice Not Reflecting in GST Portal
Ensure the customer's GSTIN is entered correctly in their profile.
Check that the invoice is marked as a Tax Invoice (not Bill of Supply or Proforma).
Make sure you have filed GSTR-1 for that return period.
IRN generation failures usually indicate an incorrect GSTIN or connectivity issue with the IRP portal.

18.3 Stock Not Updating After a Sale
Check that the item was properly linked to the invoice (not just typed as text).
Verify that the item has stock tracking enabled in the Item Master.
If using multi-warehouse, confirm you selected the correct warehouse on the invoice.

18.4 Data Sync Issues (Cloud)
Check internet connection stability.
Force-sync by going to Settings > Sync > Sync Now.
If a conflict is detected, Medisynex will show the conflict details and let you choose which version to keep.

20. Glossary of Key Terms
Term	Meaning
GSTIN	Goods and Services Tax Identification Number — 15-character unique tax ID for GST-registered businesses.
HSN Code	Harmonised System of Nomenclature — 6 to 8 digit code for goods, used to determine GST rate.
SAC Code	Services Accounting Code — similar to HSN but for services.
ITC	Input Tax Credit — GST paid on purchases that can be offset against GST collected on sales.
IRN	Invoice Reference Number — unique identifier generated by the government IRP for e-invoices.
E-Way Bill	Electronic document required for movement of goods worth over ₹50,000.
Debit Note	Document issued to increase the amount a party owes you.
Credit Note	Document issued to reduce the amount a party owes you (e.g., for returns).
FIFO	First In First Out — stock management method ensuring oldest stock is sold/used first.
TDS	Tax Deducted at Source — tax withheld at the point of payment as per Income Tax Act.
WDV	Written Down Value — depreciation method where a fixed percentage is applied on reducing book value.

function Dashboard() {
  const { businessType } = useAuth()

  switch (businessType) {
    case "PHARMACY":
      return <PharmacyDashboard />

    case "HOSPITAL":
      return <HospitalDashboard />

    case "WHOLESALER":
      return <WholesalerDashboard />

    default:
      return <DefaultDashboard />
  }
}

6.4. Real Production Approach (Better Than Switch)

In large apps, we don’t use switch-case everywhere.

✅ Use Configuration-Based Rendering
const dashboardConfig = {
  PHARMACY: {
    components: ["POS", "Inventory", "Customers"],
  },
  HOSPITAL: {
    components: ["Patients", "Billing", "Doctors"],
  },
  WHOLESALER: {
    components: ["BulkOrders", "Suppliers", "Inventory"],
  },
}

Then:

const { businessType } = useAuth()
const config = dashboardConfig[businessType]

return (
  <>
    {config.components.includes("POS") && <POS />}
    {config.components.includes("Inventory") && <Inventory />}
  </>
)

👉 This is how real SaaS products work
